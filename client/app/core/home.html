ThoughtSwap is a platform that allows facilitators to distribute prompts, and participants to contribute written thoughts in response. Facilitators can swap the thoughts so each person becomes responsible for representing someone else’s thought.

Promote Conscientious Discourse

Conscientious Discourse is the effort to maintain a practice of rigorous internal accountability while also listening and responding to other people. With an interface designed for computers, tablets and smartphones, ThoughtSwap can change the dynamic of discussion in classrooms, civic spaces and other participatory arenas.

  • Prompt Questions

    Get started quick with auto-generated participant logins. Easily manage and return to groups afterwards.

  • Contained Anonymity

    Contained Anonymity means that people can write anonymously but everyone knows that someone in the room has written each response. Personal fear of exposure is reduced but not interpersonal pro-social responsibility.

  • Thought Redistribution

    Swapping Thoughts means that people can be asked not only “What’s your opinion?” but also “What does someone else think?” Each becomes responsible for representing and elaborating someone else’s thoughts whether they agree with them or not.

  • Prompts and Responding

    Writing broadens the scope of discussion. A more diverse range of opinions in the room is presented for consideration than in traditional classroom discussion.

Share your thoughts without pressure

Take your time while everyone responds at once and form your answers to prompts without fear of judgment.

  • Complex Expression

    Sometimes, words just aren't enough. Format your thoughts as a list, picture, and more with the built-in WISYWIG editor.

  • Session History

    Keep track of any responses sent out during a session or hide the list from wondering eyes and have it erase itself on page-exit.

ThoughtSwap is an infrastructure that can deepen participation in face-to-face discourse. The point of ThoughtSwap is the face-to-face discussion, not the technology.

A typical process:

  1. A facilitator posts a prompt.

  2. Participants write for a few minutes and submit their thoughts(anonymously).

  3. The facilitator convenes a face-to-face discussion for the whole group or for small groups that uses the anonymous submitted thoughts as a spring-board.

    1. The facilitator may swap thoughts, so that each participant sees and becomes responsible for one other person’s thought.

  4. The group reports back to the whole for next steps.
